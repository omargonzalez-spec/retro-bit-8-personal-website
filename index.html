My Story

Grounded in faith.
Built by discipline.

I was raised to believe that a man's word is worth something — that if you say you'll be there, you show up, and if you take something on, you see it through. That belief has shaped everything I've built, from my faith to my family to the way I do business.

I'm a God-fearing man before I'm anything else. That's not a label I wear loosely — it's the foundation everything else stands on. Faith taught me patience when I wanted to rush, humility when I wanted credit, and conviction when it would've been easier to fold.

"I believe there's good in everyone, and I believe everyone has a purpose — mine is to help people find theirs."

Professionally, I've built my career on trust. As a health and Medicare insurance broker, I don't sell people plans — I sit with them, understand their lives, and help them choose something that actually serves them. That approach has earned me a book of nearly 185 active clients who are still with me today, plan after plan, year after year. That's not luck. That's follow-through.

Outside of work, I manage two rental properties — a quiet, steady discipline that reflects how I think: long-term, responsible, and unwilling to cut corners. I'm still the same man at home as I am in a client meeting — respectful, dependable, and serious about becoming better every year.

People & Business

Trust, measured over time.

I've spent my career in health and Medicare insurance as a broker — an industry where trust isn't optional, it's the entire job. People come to me at some of the most confusing moments of their lives, trying to make sense of coverage that affects their health and their finances. I don't rush that conversation.

That approach has built a book of business of roughly 185 active clients — people who are still on plans I helped them choose, year after year. They stay because I show up, I follow through, and I tell them the truth even when it's not what they want to hear.

Alongside that, I own and manage two rental properties. It's a different kind of business, but the same mindset: long-term thinking, responsibility, and a refusal to cut corners.

Leadership Mindset

Strategic. Decisive. Grounded.

I'm wired to see the bigger picture, make a decision, and move — that's simply how I'm built. I'd rather take responsibility and act than wait for permission or perfect conditions. But I've learned that leadership without humility just becomes noise. The kind of leadership I want to practice is the kind that helps people move forward, not the kind that makes them feel small.

Life Outside Business

Work is what I do. Family is why I do it.

None of this means much to me if it doesn't translate into showing up for the people I love. Family comes first — always has. I make time for the people who matter, and I try to be fully present when I'm with them, not half-checked-out thinking about the next client call.

My friendships matter to me too. I believe in loyalty — the kind that shows up in slow seasons, not just the good ones. I want the people around me to feel supported, valued, and genuinely enjoyed, not just tolerated.

At the end of the day, I want to be known less for what I built and more for how I made the people around me feel — respected, valued, and never alone.
